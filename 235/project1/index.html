Beloved Fisherman Tragically Dead

17 October 2003

A local Queenstown fisherman, Ronan Owens, was found dead this morning after not returning home and his boat not returning to the dock. The details remain fuzzy.

Ronan was a family man who worked tirelessly to provide not just for his family but for the community. He dedicated his life to fishing locally, however on many occasions he would spend his free time fishing on open oceans with his family's boat. He also enjoyed hunting expensive and rare fish, including swordfish (his family notes his swordfish tacos were phenominal), tuna, and seabass.
Sebastian, one of Ronan's good friends, found Ronan this morning. Sebastian recounted to news-sources and the police that Ronan did not text him last night after saying to Sebastian in the morning that he was "out on open water again, seeing if [he] can catch something big :)". Sebastian initially became curious when the next morning, Ronan's wife called him with a little concern. He headed out to the open water docks where he found Ronan's boat slightly away from the dock with the sail down and anchor dropped. When he asked for a ride to get closer to the boat, he found Ronan face-up laying down in the middle of the boat with multiple lacerations and gashes in his stomach.
There is suspected foul play with this scene, considering the multiple lacerations that, on police's first glance, were "not caused by anything human-made". Questions still remain regarding motive and method, the police chief said: "this man had no history of transgressing on anyone and was beloved in the community. We will continue to investigate until cause of death has been determined and if foul play is involved."
The police have urged the media to let people know about the homicide and death tip lines. You can find those on the purple addendum.
